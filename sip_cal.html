Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is SIP?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. It helps investors build wealth through disciplined and long-term investing.

How is SIP maturity amount calculated?

SIP maturity amount is calculated using the future value formula, considering the monthly investment amount, expected annual return and investment period.

Is SIP better than Fixed Deposit (FD)?

SIPs offer higher long-term growth potential compared to fixed deposits, but mutual fund investments are subject to market risks while FDs provide fixed returns.

Can I withdraw my SIP anytime?

Yes, most open-ended mutual funds allow redemption at any time. However, some funds may have an exit load if redeemed before a specified period.

Is SIP taxable?

Yes. The gains earned from mutual funds are taxable. Tax treatment depends on whether the fund is equity-oriented or debt-oriented and on the holding period.

What is the minimum amount required to start a SIP?

Most mutual funds allow investors to start a SIP with as little as ₹100 or ₹500 per month.

Can I increase my SIP amount later?

Yes. Investors can increase their monthly contribution through a Step-Up SIP facility offered by most mutual funds.

Which is better: SIP or Lumpsum investment?

SIP is suitable for regular investors and helps average out market fluctuations, whereas lumpsum investments may be suitable when a large amount is available for investment.

What rate of return should I assume while using the SIP Calculator?

Equity mutual funds have historically delivered returns ranging from 10% to 15% annually, though actual returns may vary depending on market conditions.

Can SIP help me become a crore-pati?

Yes. Long-term disciplined investing through SIPs can help create a corpus of ₹1 crore or more, depending on the investment amount, duration and returns earned.
