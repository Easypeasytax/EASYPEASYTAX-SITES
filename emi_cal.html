Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount paid every month to repay a loan. It consists of both principal and interest components.

How is EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the reducing balance method based on the loan amount, interest rate and loan tenure. Banks and NBFCs in India follow this method.

Does prepayment reduce EMI?

Yes. Prepayment can either reduce your monthly EMI or shorten the loan tenure, thereby reducing the total interest payable.

Which loan has the lowest interest rate?

Home loans generally have the lowest interest rates, followed by car loans. Personal loans usually carry higher interest rates.

Can I increase my EMI amount?

Yes. Paying a higher EMI helps reduce the overall interest burden and allows you to repay the loan faster.

Is EMI calculated on simple interest?

No. Most banks and financial institutions calculate EMI using the reducing balance method instead of simple interest.

Can I prepay my home loan?

Yes. Most banks allow home loan prepayment without any penalty, helping borrowers save on interest costs.

Which is better: shorter tenure or lower EMI?

A shorter tenure reduces the total interest paid, whereas a longer tenure lowers the monthly EMI burden. The choice depends on your repayment capacity.

Can I get a tax benefit on home loan EMI?

Yes. Under the Income Tax Act, borrowers may claim deductions on principal repayment under Section 80C and on interest paid under Section 24(b), subject to applicable limits.

Does increasing the down payment reduce EMI?

Yes. A higher down payment reduces the loan amount, which in turn lowers the monthly EMI and total interest payable.
