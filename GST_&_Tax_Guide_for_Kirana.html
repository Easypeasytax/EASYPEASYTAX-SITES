GST & Tax Guide for Kirana (General Store) Owners

Complete GST & Income Tax guide for kirana and general store owners with HSN codes, GST rates, compliance rules in English, Hindi & Hinglish.

By EasyPeasyTax тАв

Running a kirana (general) store is about more than just serving your neighbourhood with daily-use goods. It also means staying compliant with GST and income tax rules.

With GST rate rationalisation effective from 22 September 2025, small traders must clearly understand which taxes apply, when registration is required, and how to stay compliant.

ЁЯз╛ What Is GST for Kirana Merchants?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a tax on the supply of goods and services, collected at each stage of the supply chain.

If your annual turnover crosses the GST threshold, you must register and file GST returns, even if most of the products you sell are taxed at lower rates or are Nil-rated.

ЁЯУМ GST Rate Structure (Post 2025 Rationalisation)

To simplify compliance, the GST Council has focused on fewer major tax slabs.

ЁЯФ╣ 0% GST (Nil / Exempt Items)

  • Unbranded food grains like rice, atta and pulses
  • Fresh fruits and vegetables
  • Unprocessed milk, eggs and curd
  • Certain essential medicines and health-related supplies
Many everyday kirana essentials remain Nil-rated or exempt under GST.

ЁЯФ╣ 5% GST (Lower Rate Items)

Several commonly sold grocery and household items generally fall under the 5% GST category, subject to branding and packaging conditions:

  • Packaged food items (snacks, biscuits, namkeen)
  • Soap, toothpaste and shampoo
  • Packaged edible oils and dry fruits
  • Tea and coffee (packed)

This lower rate reduces tax burden and simplifies pricing for kirana merchants.

ЁЯФ╣ 18% GST (Standard Rate)

  • Small electrical or electronic appliances
  • Some packaged consumer goods beyond daily essentials
  • Services, if provided and registered under GST

ЁЯФ╣ 28% / Higher GST (Luxury or Sin Goods)

Items like tobacco products and certain luxury goods attract higher GST rates (along with compensation cess, where applicable).

These are usually not relevant for most kirana shops, but awareness helps avoid classification mistakes.

ЁЯПк Do Kirana Merchants Need GST Registration?

GST registration becomes mandatory if:

  • Annual turnover exceeds тВ╣40 lakh (goods only)
  • Lower limits apply in case of services or special category states

ЁЯУК Benefits of Understanding GST Rates Correctly

  • Easier compliance and fewer mistakes
  • Correct pricing of goods
  • Avoidance of penalties and notices
  • Better accounting and record-keeping

ЁЯз╛ GST Filing тАУ Basic Compliance Rules

Once registered, a kirana merchant must:

  • File monthly or quarterly GST returns (as applicable)
  • File annual GST return

Records to maintain:

  • Sales invoices
  • Purchase bills
  • Credit and debit notes
  • GST payment challans

ЁЯТ░ Income Tax for Kirana Shop Owners

Apart from GST, kirana merchants must comply with income tax laws.

ЁЯФ╣ Presumptive Taxation тАУ Section 44AD

  • Profit presumed at 8% of cash turnover
  • 6% of digital turnover
  • No requirement of detailed books

тЪая╕П Common Mistakes Kirana Merchants Should Avoid

  • Ignoring GST registration threshold
  • Selling goods without proper invoices
  • Mixing personal and business bank accounts
  • Delay or non-filing of returns
  • Ignoring notices from tax authorities

ЁЯз╛ Kirana merchants ke liye GST kya hai?

GST goods aur services ki supply par lagne wala tax hai, jo supply chain ke har stage par collect hota hai.

Agar aapka annual turnover GST limit cross karta hai, toh GST registration aur return filing mandatory ho jati hai, chahe zyada items Nil-rated hi kyun na ho.
